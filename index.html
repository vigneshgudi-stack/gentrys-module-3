05

What the market & incentives tell us

↳ Q9 · Pricing

Priced in range, not at a premium

At a 6.1% in-place / 6.82% pro-forma cap, Gentry's sits in the middle of the St. Louis market, not below it. There's no scarcity premium baked in to defend — which means the conservative case supports a price conversation, not the ask.

↳ Q3 · The twin

Buy the one that's alive

The $195M next door buys a vacant gut at ~$470k/door. Gentry's delivers the same building, leased and earning, improvable at ~$25k/door. The contrast is the single clearest argument for the basis.

↳ Q12 · Stack the shelters

QOZ is confirmed; historic is the upside

The Opportunity Zone is locked in the OM. The open lever is historic tax credits — Mansion House (National Register-listed) is funding its rebuild with them. Whether Gentry's qualifies as a certified historic structure is a high-value diligence item worth ~25–40% on eligible costs.

↳ Q10 · Q13 · TIF reality

Abatement beats fresh TIF here

Downtown has multiple existing TIF districts, but a new TIF must clear a but-for test and Aldermanic approval — slow for a renovation. Chapter 353 abatement (up to 25 years) and the SLDC scorecard are the more realistic levers for an occupied value-add.
